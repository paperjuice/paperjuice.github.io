About me

My name is Dragos Dumitru, born and raised in Bucharest, Romania.

I have finished a high-school focused on math and informatics and right after I passed my final exams, I applied for a Computer Games Design course at university in UK. The reason why I chose a game design course over a BSc degrees such as computer sciences is because I wanted to get closer to developing games and I was a huge fan of this type of entertainment for more than a decade.

Firstly, I personally believe that people tend to flatten out their existence constantly living in the same loop constrained by this vicious cycle in which most of their time is spend working today in order to be able to quietly live tomorrow. Being caught in such life doesn’t necessarily mean that the individuals feel sad, but having the same experience every day, constantly can just translate as monotonous. Video games seem like a gateway, ironically, to escape reality in order to experience strong, deep and real feelings. These experiences cannot often arise in day by day activities but video games can generate them with ease if developed right. Seeing people getting excited by something I’ve created is priceless.

Secondly, being able to bring to life worlds, characters, events is mesmerizing. Any piece of art, with the right script inside the right engine can be the beginning of a wonderful creation. Games bring together individuals, create relationships and help people briefly disconnect from reality and escape in fantastic places.

During university I focused myself on the principles of good design in video games while continuing to improve and develop my programming skills. When it comes to designing, my main focus is how I can create a fun and immersive game-play. Because I am a huge fan of RPG, I always look into how I can make the player feel powerful while maintaining a fair and challenging experience. Because I have a background in C++, I was taught to look into what would be the most efficient solution for that particular problem. I try to write robust and clean code in order to make it easy to read and facilitate possible refactoring at later times. Because optimising games is a huge part of the game development (in order to make the product available for as many devices/machines as possible) I also developed games for mobile. The phone I had at that time wasn’t very powerful so getting a good frame-rate was something I took in consideration right when I was starting any new project.

Two months after I got to university in UK I managed to get my first job abroad. The reason for the rush was because I like to have full control over my life. With a job in hand I could manage me and my finances and get a better understanding of what it means to fully support myself.

The first job I had was in a bar. I have some funny experiences there to tell but it wasn’t for me. Two months later I got another job at a big company. Unfortunately for me, big company means many, many desks and, as you probably are guessing, I was there to clean and vacuum the place. The job provided me with good income and I could easily do my university work, as well.

Eight~ months later, two representatives from Derivco, an international software development company, came to university looking for student that are suited and want to apply for an internship over the summer period. I pushed a bit and I asked them if they would consider anyone for a part-time position. They told me that they haven’t done this before but are open to have such offer for the right student. I applied for a part-time position and in February I received an e-mail from them saying that they would like to have me for an interview. As an outcome to the interview, they liked my enthusiasm and eagerness to learn and self-develop and so I became the first part-time employee at the company and still am fourteen months later.

The Derivco experience had an incredible positive impact on my development as a professional. I’ve learned and performed under real-life working methodologies, improved my communication and programming skills immensely as well as gaining brand new skills and knowledge about all sorts of tools required to complete my tasks.

After a year working as a QA automation test analyst at Derivco, because of my fast learning ability and enthusiasm for new technologies, I was proposed to join a new team as a software developer.

The new position require me to learn SQL and a new programming language called Elixir. I find this very exciting especially because it is out of my comfort zone. My mind is set on an object oriented programming way and getting to learn a functional programming language is something new and interesting. Expanding my knowledge in different sectors will be beneficial when faced with solving all sort of programming problems.

At the moment I am focusing on my dissertation project as well as getting confident with Elixir.

I am a huge fan of RPGs, especially if they offer high level of difficulty. As a result, for my dissertation I am working on a game that combines elements seen in the rogue-like genre, inspired by Diablo aesthetics and camera perspective and mixed with Dark Souls game-play.
